Visual Design Submission - Layla Salhab

Pages:

  • For the mock-ups, I decided to create visuals for the website of the ficitonal gallery that this project revolves around. The gallery name is fictitious, and is called Wild Thyme Project.
  • Overall look: I have gone for a minimalist yet retro-digital look by using neon colours, geometric shapes and strong lines, and an "early internet"-looking font called Silom for the gallery name.
  • Typefaces chosen: I chose Silom for my logo/gallery name as it stands out in style and the rest of the mock-ups use Epilogue in a variety of Semi-bold, light and italic. It is a modern sans-serif font which I found to be a nice pairing with the gallery logo.
  • Background image: I have created a background image (see Image 7 in my Photo gallery) which would act as the background image for all pages other than the main landing page. This would allow a differenciation of the content pages to the main page of the website.
  • The colour palette for this entire website is a mix of a strong primary blue, neon green, pink and black and white:
    • White #FFFFFF
    • Black #000000
    • Blue #0031FF
    • Pink #FFACE2
    • Green #E0FF71

Mockup 1: Home Page

Link to mock-ups

This first mock-up is the home page.
I created a soft shadow at the top of the page to indicate the header in a subtle way.
The logo sits on the left, and a search bar with a menu hamburger sits on the right. This creates a simple but straightforward header that allows users to view the different menu items as well as look for specific words or information with the search bar.
The main page has 3 content boxes with a coloured border. The boxes would be moved around by the user with their mouse if the website was live.
Each of the boxes holds different content that would be the latest news or the most relevant to users, such as "What's on", "Open Calls" and "Past Exhibitions".
I then added 2 green neon lines to show a separation of the content before the blue footer.

Mockup 2: Press Page

Link to mock-ups

This second mock-up is a content page, the Press page.
The header and footer are the same as the landing page for better clarity and continuity
This page has two lines underneath the page title that are in symmetry with the neon green ones at the bottom of the page.
There is a filter on the left to filter press articles according to their date or any specific words.
I have added a number box that users would use to navigate between the different pages of the Press page. When clicked, the number box would turn pink to signal to the user where they are.

Mockup 3: Events Page

Link to mock-ups

This third mock-up is a content page, the "What's On" page.
The header and footer are the same as the landing page for better clarity and continuity
This page also has two lines underneath the page title that are in symmetry with the neon green ones at the bottom of the page.
I have edited 4 images (see in Photos) that would act as the cover image for each of these 4 sub-categories: "Open Calls", "Events", "Family Fun", "Library Facilities". The rest are image placeholders for anything else that would go under the "What's On" page.
I chose images that would be descriptive enough and relevant to their respective sub-categories to give users an idea of what to expect by simply looking at the image.
